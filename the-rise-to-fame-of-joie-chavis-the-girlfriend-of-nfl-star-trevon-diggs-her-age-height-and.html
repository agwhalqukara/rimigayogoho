High School Football Career

Metro team twice.

College Football Career

After his impressive high school career, Diggs was offered a scholarship to play collegiate football at the University of Alabama. He played for Alabama in 2016 as a true freshman, showcasing his versatility by playing as a return specialist, wide receiver, and safety.

He concluded the season with 11 catches for 88 yards and a score on offense, five tackles, and one forced fumble on defense, and 296 total return yards on special teams.

In 2017, Diggs shifted to cornerback permanently and started the first game of the season against Florida State University. He continued to impress on the field, making six tackles and intercepting three passes. He also participated in special teams, returning two kickoffs for 74 yards and 18 punts for 154 yards.

Diggs suffered a foot injury during his junior year in 2018, which saw him missing the rest of the season after starting the first six games. Despite his injury, he finished the season with 20 tackles and a pick-six.

During his senior year in 2019, Diggs started 12 games and had an exceptional season, finishing with 37 tackles, three interceptions (tied for second on the team), eight passes defended (tied for first on the team), and two recovered fumbles.
