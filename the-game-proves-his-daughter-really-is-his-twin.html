The Game Proves His Daughter Really Is His 'Twin'

 Posted on July 9, 2024  |  3 minutes  |  439 words  |  Aldo Pusey

The Game has a way of getting people talking — and this time, it’s about his daughter, whom he referred to as “his twin” in Instagram posts.

The “How We Do” rapper took to Instagram on Sunday (April 9) to share a series of photos with 13-year-old Cali Lynn Dream Taylor, his daughter with ex Tiffney Cambridge.

“We not out here calling each other “Twin” cause it sound kool…. We really do this shhhhh,” he captioned the three photos.

Though The Game’s relationship with Cambridge didn’t work out despite three seasons of the hit VH1 show, Marrying The Game, the rapper (real name Jayceon Terrell Taylor) hasn’t given up on love.

Last January, the Compton, California native posted a message on his Instagram Story to any woman that’s willing to spend the rest of her life with him. In his Story, The Game kept it very simple: he’s ready to put a ring on it and all that needs to happen is for the person to show him they’re ready.

“I’m putting a ring on it this year all you gotta do is show up for me like I show up for you & pick a date,” he wrote in his Story.

The Shade Room reshared the post on its page, and several commented on it, mostly dragging The Game for sounding desperate for love on his social media. The rapper caught wind of all the commotion and let fans know to be more positive, especially with the new year in full effect.

“The opinions of people who have never met a person in real life be wild,” he wrote. “It’s usually always people who do not like what they see in the mirror. It’s the Lord’s day… let’s rejoice in his name & be what we said we were going to be this year. Stop acting like somebody stole your Amazon package & be happy we were blessed with another day of life today.”

Last month, The Game was on the search for love when he put out a tweet in reference to Yung Miami being taken off the market in her alleged relationship with Diddy. Game wasted no time asking followers to help him find a “Yung Atlanta,” “Yung Houston” or “Yung New York.”
