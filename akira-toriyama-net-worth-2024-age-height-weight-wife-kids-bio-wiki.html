Early Life & Biography:

Akira Toriyama was born in a village called Nagoya, Aichi, Japan, on 5 April 1955. His father had an auto repair business and enjoyed motorbike racing.

Soon after that, he realized his skills when he started drawing pictures of his friends, and after winning a prize at a local art studio. Thus, he got interested in the work.

In 1974, he joined the high school to study art, specializing in publicity, which, in 1977 he left to enroll in the ‘cartoon drawing’ course.

Career:

Akira Toriyama is famous for being illustrator; Manga and game artists are best known for his work the Manga series “Dr. Slump” from 1980 until 1984 and “Dragon Ball” from 1984 until 1995.

He went to the Industrial Higher School in 1973, and immediately, he was hired by an advertising company in Nagoya, where he worked for three years, but his lack of interest made him ditch his job. His mangaka life started with a publication in Shonen Jump. Later he created many other stories for that magazine and collected them in volumes. Toriyama received immense stardom in Japan and abroad, thanks to his work Dragon Ball, which has been seen by more than three generations worldwide.

Many people may not know, but before becoming a manga artist, he worked at an advertising agency in Nagoya designing posters for three years. Following his success, Toriyama has built his studio is called Bird Studio, which is a play on his name, “tori,” meaning “bird.” Toriyama does nearly all of the work at Bird Studio himself.

He draws inspiration from cartoonist Osamu Tezuka, Walt Disney’s ‘101 Dalmatians,’ and says that Jackie Chan’s movie ‘Drunken Master’ was the inspiration behind his manga ‘Dragon Ball.
