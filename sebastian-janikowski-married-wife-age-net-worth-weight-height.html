Who is Sebastian Janikowski?

Sebastian Paweł Janikowski was born in Wałbrzych, Poland on March 2, 1978 to parents Henryk Janikowski and Halina Janikowski. His father was a professional soccer player in his home country of Poland, but he decided to move to the United States in the early 1980s in search of a better life for himself and his family and hopefully the, to revive his career in football. A few years after moving to the United States, Janikowski’s father divorced his mother to marry an American. While staying with his mother, Sabastian also developed a strong passion for football and he did well to hone his skills to the point of mastering it. This massive development in the sport earned him a spot in the Poland U17 team at the age of 15.

Luckily for Sebastian, his father’s union with a woman from the United States gave him the opportunity to move there as a legal resident. His arrival in the United States was quite difficult at first as he spoke very little English, but over time he got used to the environment and improved his English by watching TV shows and attending to a three-week evening course.

Sebastian was selected as part of the Orangewood Christian football team, although he was lucky enough to only play five games with the team, helped him qualify for the state championship of class A after scoring a record 15 goals. However, his team ultimately lost 3-2 to Lakeland Christian via penalty, he was hailed as a vital part of the team’s commendable performance. Still residing with his father and stepmother in Orlando, Florida, he later signed up for the Orlando Lions under-19 football team, coached by Angello Rossi. Rossi, who saw great potential in Sebastian, convinced his father, Henryk, to allow him to transfer to Seabreeze High School located in Daytona Beach, where Rossi was also a coach.

He performed incredibly well for the Seabreeze football team as a placekicker and was also part of the school football team. Some of his exploits in his high school football game include; one-for-four 50+ yard, 60 yard, and 82 yard strike. For his college education and college career, Sebastian decided to pitch his tent at Florida State University, where he played for theThe Florida State Seminoles football team, coached by Bobby Bowden. In 1999, he received for the second consecutive year the Lou Louza Award, which recognizes the best college kicker in the country.

Professional career

After graduating from college, Sebastian Janikowski had plenty of scout pitching to get him on his team, which was eventually drafted by the Oakland Raiders. This draft made history as he became the fourth NFL player to enter the first round of the draft as the 17th pick in the 2000 NFL Draft. On September 12, Sebastian tied the record of the NFL established by Tom Dempsey in 1970 as well as Jason Elam in 1998 after scoring for 63 yards in the rainy first half against the Denver Broncos.

Two months later (November 27, 2011), he broke the team record for most goals in a single game after scoring 6 goals – 40, 47, 42, 19, 37 and 44 yards in the game against the Chicago Bears. By the 2017 season, he had played a total of 268 games with the raider for which he held the record for most games played.
