Snow White is the titular character of Disney’s first animated feature film, Snow White and the Seven Dwarfs. It was an American film that made a debut on the 4th of February 1938 (United States).

As per the source, it is based on the German fairy tale by the Brothers Grimm. David Hand remained the supervising director. Moreover, the various directors named Larry Morey, Perce Pearce, William Cottrell, Wilfred Jackson, and Ben Sharpsteen worked on the film’s individual sequences.

Walt Disney – Snow White and the Seven Dwarfs.

On December 21, 1937, the movie came out at the Carthay Circle Theatre. In fact, Snow White and the Seven Dwarfs shortly held the record of highest-grossing sound film at the time after earning $8 million USD.

In 1938, it also received the honor of getting nominated for Best Musical Score at the Academy Awards. The producer also awarded an honorary Oscar for the film the next year in 1939.

Early Life, Parents & Brief Life Story

The princess was born into the royal family. Snow Whites’ mother “Queen Eva” died in childbirth shortly after giving birth to the princess. A year later, her father “the King” married again and his new wife “The Evil Queen” was also a very beautiful woman.

However, she was a wicked lady and practiced witchcraft. Snow’s dad also died suspiciously after marrying his second spouse. She had a magic mirror who always told her that she is the fairest woman in the world.

The evil queen used to get extremely happy as the magic mirror never lied. On the other hand, her step-daughter grew up and became more beautiful each day. Therefore, the Magic Mirror then started to tell her that Snow White is the fairest.

The second queen later ordered a huntsman to take Snow into the forest in order to kill her. The queen also demanded her heart so that she could consume it and became more beautiful.

But the huntsman spared her life and took the heart of a wild animal instead. Then, her step-mother asked the same question to the mirror. To her surprise, the magic mirror replied Snow is the fairest.

Later, she became furious and decided to kill the girl herself. Meanwhile, the princess took a refugee at a tiny cottage that belonged to a group of seven dwarfs. The queen disguised herself as a farmer’s wife and went to the cottage.

She offered her a poisoned apple which made the young girl unconscious. Assuming she was dead, dwarves put her in a glass casket. A prince stumbled upon the innocent princess and was able to revive her in the end. He even proposed her for marriage which she accepted.
