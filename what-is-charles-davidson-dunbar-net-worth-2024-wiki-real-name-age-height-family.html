FAQs about Charles Davidson Dunbar

Who was Charles Davidson Dunbar?

He was a renowned military piper and the first pipe major commissioned as a pipe officer in Britain and the British Empire. He was noted for his service with the Seaforth Highlanders and later the Gordon Highlanders.

When and where was he born?

He was born on July 17, 1870, in Halkirk, Scotland.

What were some of his military achievements?

Dunbar joined the British Army as a piper and rose to prominence, eventually becoming a pipe major. He was awarded the Distinguished Conduct Medal (DCM) for his service.

Did he serve in any notable battles?

Yes, he served in World War I and was significantly involved in various campaigns, notably piping his troops into battle, which was highly dangerous due to the advent of trench warfare and machine guns.

How did he contribute to pipe music?

Dunbar was a celebrated piper who participated in many competitions and was highly respected for his skill and contributions to military and Highland piping music.

Where did he live and work post-military?

After retiring from the military, he emigrated to Canada where he continued to be involved with the piping community, particularly in Ontario.

What was significant about his appointment as a pipe officer?

His commission was unique as he was the first pipe major in the history of British and Canadian forces to hold a commission while retaining command of a pipe band.

Did he have any family?

He was married to Margaret Dolina Murray, and they had several children together. His family was deeply involved in his life, and his sons were taught to play the pipes, although none reached his level of mastery.
